ARGENTINE CONGRESS ON AGROINFORMATICS

Llamado a presentación de trabajos - Chamada de Trabalhos - Call for papers

 

The 13th Argentine Congress on Agroinformatics calls for Researchers, Technologists, Developers, Entrepreneurs and Companies related to the agribusiness sector to send papers related to ICTs applied to agricultural, agroindustrial and environmental issues, ranging from experimental to commercial instances.

The agro-industrial sector needs more and better technological solutions to its own problems and those derived from economic to climatic changes, solutions that in turn can be useful to other countries. Many of the solutions to these problems emerge from the contact between researchers, entrepreneurs, entrepreneurs and students whose ideas and developments complement each other.

Topics of interest

The suggested topics, with specific application to agricultural, agroindustrial and environmental problems, although not exclusive, are the following:

·         Software and agricultural information systems.

·         Modeling of production systems.

·         Integration and traceability of agro-industrial chains.

·         Geomatics, Geographic Information Systems, IDEs, Remote Sensing and Earth Observation.

·         Agro-industrial robotics.

·         Precision agriculture and livestock.

·         Sensor networks in crops, dairy, feed-lots and processing plants.

·         Embedded systems and electronic developments in the agro-industry.

·         Environmental monitoring and control.

·         Ontologies, Big Data, Open Data, DataMining and artificial intelligence applied to agriculture.

·         Bioinformatics and biological records.

·         Agroindustrial Web Services and Web 2.0.

·         New developments and experiments in AgroTICs.

·         Mobile apps.

·         Internet of things applied to agriculture.

·         Educational experiences in ICTs applied to agriculture.

 

Papers categories and Requested Formats

The papers submitted must be related to the reference topics and present results of research tasks, progress reports or specific developments. Whether due to its originality or potential application and impact on agribusiness or the public sector, the work must be of interest to the different actors of technological innovation and production. The following types of work will be accepted

- Full papers: papers with original and / or novel contributions, with a maximum length of 14 pages. It must contain the following points:

  • Title: Must represent the content with, if possible, no more than 10 words. Use capital letters only for proper names and for the beginning of the sentence. If necessary, a subtitle of similar length can be added.

  • Authors: Surname and Name, institution, city, country and email contact of each author.

  • Abstract: up to 200 words.

  • Include three to five key words using the language of the text.

  • Introduction

  • Paper Body: it is suggested: data and methodology, results and discussion.

  • Conclusions

  • Annexes (if  needed)

  • References (do not include references that have not been cited or mentioned in the paper)

- Short papers: papers with original and / or novel contributions, that does not merit the writing of an extended work, with a maximum length of 4 pages. It must contain the following points:

  • Title: Must represent the content with, if possible, no more than 10 words. Use capital letters only for proper names and for the beginning of the sentence. If necessary, a subtitle of similar length can be added.

  • Authors: Surname and Name, institution, city, country and email contact of each author.

  • Abstract: up to 200 words.

  • Include three to five keywords using the language of the text.

  • Introduction

  • Paper Body: it is suggested: data and methodology, results, and discussion.

  • Conclusions (may contain future research)

  • Annexes (if needed)

  • References (do not include references that have not been cited or mentioned in the paper)

- Data papers: The objective of this category is to present datasets generated for research that are relevant to the symposium focusing specifically on describing the dataset and its accessibility. It provides the authors with the possibility of being able to give credit, to the creators of the data, through citations and peer review and, in addition, to contribute to the opening of data and the reuse of the data set. It must contain the following points, with a maximum length of 14 pages:

  • Title: Must represent the content with, if possible, no more than 10 words. Use capital letters only for proper names and for the beginning of the sentence. If necessary, a subtitle of similar length can be added.

  • Authors: Surname and Name, institution, city, country and email contact of each author.

  • Abstract: up to 200 words.

  • Include three to five keywords using the language of the text.

  • Introduction

  • Paper Body: The work must provide details such as i) the context of the data collection, ii) the choice of the software environment, iii) the data processing decisions, iv) the file formats, v) the updating of the data and vi ) license of use and access.

  • Annexes (if needed)

  • References (do not include references that have not been cited or mentioned in the paper)

- Extended abstracts: Extended summary that provides an opportunity for researchers and professionals to present and discuss their most recent advances, ideas, ongoing research, experiences and challenges on the subject. It will have a maximum length of 2 pages, including the following points:

  • Title: Must represent the content with, if possible, no more than 10 words. Use capital letters only for proper names and for the beginning of the sentence. If necessary, a subtitle of similar length can be added.

  • Authors: Surname and Name, institution, city, country and email contact of each author.

  • Paper Body: extended abstract, can include one figure.

  • Include three to five keywords using the language of the text.

  • References (if it is mentioned, do not include into references text that has not been cited or mentioned in paper)

For all four types of papers, works submitted must not have been published before, nor should they be reviewed at another conference, symposium or journal. The four types of papers will be included in the proceedings of the congress.

Demonstrations: the demonstrations will present research project results (and thus obtain feedback from potential users), as well as the development of particularly innovative applications or processes in private companies or by independent professionals (and thus exchange experiences acquired in practice). These works will be included in the proceedings of the congress.

  • Title: Must represent the content with, if possible, no more than 10 words. Use capital letters only for proper names and for the beginning of the sentence. If necessary, a subtitle of similar length can be added.

  • Authors: Surname and Name, institution, city, country and email contact of each author.

  • Paper Body: extended abstract up to 2 pages (can include one figure) with detail in: application or process that include: a short description and its objectives, problems  it solves, audience of users to whom it is intended, and technology used and (ii) a URL of a demo version, if applicable, if it can be run online or, alternatively, a URL with a video that shows the main features of the demo.

  • Include three to five key words using the language of the text.

  • References (if it is mentioned, do not include into references text that has not been cited or mentioned in paper)

Oral Communications: papers already presented at an international congress or published in a relevant journal during the years 2018 to 2020  are received whose length does not exceed one page. Accepted Oral Communications will have a space for their presentation in the congress program, with the aim of disseminating in the national scope works already published in the international scope. It should be clarified that, because these works have already been published previously, the proceedings of the congress will only include their abstracts.

  • Title: Must represent the content with, if possible, no more than 10 words. Use capital letters only for proper names and for the beginning of the sentence. If necessary, a subtitle of similar length can be added.

  • Authors: Surname and Name, institution, city, country and email contact of each author.

  • Abstract: extended up to 1 page (can include one figure).  Must include original information (event/journal, date, URL access or DOI)

  • Include three to five keywords using the language of the text.

  • References (if it is mentioned, do not include into references text that has not been cited or mentioned in paper)

Papers must be written in Spanish, Portuguese or English and must be on LNCS (Lecture Notes in Computer Science) format. The template provided by Springer must be used, it can be downloaded here.

All papers must be submitted in PDF format, without authors, and without affiliation until the review process is finished. Abstracts should have sufficient space to include, in their final version, the authors and their affiliation. Sending papers to any of the 49 JAIIO symposiums is done through the SADIO conference management system. To submit your work you need to be registered (or register) and access the system. To answer any questions, you have the instructions for obtaining credentials and access to the job submission system. The works will be carefully reviewed by the members of the Scientific Committee, taking into account their originality, contribution, technical quality, and clarity. The best papers will be invited for publication in the Electronic Magazine of SADIO.

At least one of the authors of the approved works must be registered at the conference before the deadline for the final paper presentation (camera-ready) in order for it to be incorporated into the proceedings of the event. It is also a requirement to present at the date and time indicated for the exhibition of the work. Please note that work whose author does not attend to exhibit it will be removed from the proceedings. In the case of force majeure, the author may send a representative (duly authorized) to present it in his name.
